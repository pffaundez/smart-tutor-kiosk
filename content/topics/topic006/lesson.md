Large Language Models, often called LLMs, are AI systems that work with human language. They are designed to read, generate, and predict text. Tools like ChatGPT are based on Large Language Models.

An LLM learns from a very large collection of text. This text can include books, websites, articles, and conversations. During training, the model studies patterns in language. It learns how words and sentences are usually connected.

The model does not understand language like a person does. Instead, it predicts which word is most likely to come next based on patterns it learned during training. This is similar to a very advanced autocomplete system.

When you ask an LLM a question, the model analyzes your input and generates a response one token at a time. A token is usually a word or part of a word. Each new token is chosen based on probability.

Large Language Models can perform many tasks. They can summarize text, answer questions, translate languages, write code, and help with learning. Their flexibility comes from training on many different examples of language.

The quality of an LLM depends on several factors. These include the amount of training data, the design of the model, and the training process. Better training data often leads to more reliable responses.

LLMs can still make mistakes. Sometimes they generate incorrect information or responses that sound confident but are not accurate. This is often called hallucination.

Even with these limitations, Large Language Models have become an important part of modern AI systems. They are widely used in education, customer support, software tools, and creative applications.
