# What is a Neural Network?

A neural network is a type of machine learning model inspired by how the human brain processes information. It is made of simple units called neurons, which are connected to each other and organized into layers. Neural networks are widely used in artificial intelligence to recognize patterns in data, such as images, text, or sound.

Each neuron receives one or more input values, multiplies them by weights, adds them together, and then applies a mathematical function called an activation function. The result is passed to the next layer of neurons. By combining many neurons, a neural network can model complex relationships between inputs and outputs.

Neural networks typically have three main types of layers. The input layer receives the raw data, such as pixel values from an image. One or more hidden layers process this data by transforming it into more abstract representations. The output layer produces the final result, such as a classification or prediction.

The power of neural networks comes from training. During training, the network is shown many examples with known correct answers. The model makes a prediction, compares it to the correct answer, and measures the error. This error is then used to adjust the weights of the connections, usually through a method called backpropagation. Over time, the network learns weights that reduce the error and improve performance.

It is important to note that neural networks do not “understand” data in a human sense. They do not reason or think consciously. Instead, they detect statistical patterns in the data they are trained on. If the training data is biased, incomplete, or noisy, the neural network’s predictions may also be unreliable.

Neural networks are the foundation of many modern AI systems, including image recognition, speech recognition, language translation, and recommendation systems. However, they require large amounts of data and computing power, and their decisions can be difficult to interpret.
