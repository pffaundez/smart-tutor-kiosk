A neural network is a machine learning model inspired by how the human brain processes information. It is built from simple units called neurons, which are connected in layers and work together to recognize patterns in data.

Each neuron takes input values, combines them using weights, and passes the result forward. During training, the network adjusts these weights by comparing its predictions with correct answers. Over time, this allows the model to improve its performance.

Neural networks are widely used in AI tasks such as image recognition, speech processing, and language translation. However, they do not think or understand like humans—they learn statistical patterns from data, and their accuracy depends strongly on the quality of that data.
