Generative AI refers to AI systems that can create new content such as text, images, audio, video, or code. Instead of only predicting labels, these models generate outputs that can look like they were made by humans or real-world processes. At a high level, generative AI learns patterns from large datasets and then produces new examples by predicting what should come next (such as the next word in a sentence) until a complete output is formed.

Generative AI usually does not copy and paste training data in normal use. Instead, it learns statistical relationships that let it combine concepts in new ways, although it can sometimes reproduce patterns it has seen before. Generative AI is useful for drafting and exploring ideas, but it also has risks: it can produce plausible but incorrect information, reflect bias, or generate unsafe content if not constrained. For many applications, it is important to use guardrails such as clear prompts, restricted sources, verification, and human oversight.
